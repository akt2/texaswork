Major Cities Job Markets

Different job categories have led in different regions of our diverse state. State-wide, job growth as measured by hourly wage has been driven most by food, transportation, sales, office administration, and education. The top-paying jobs remain within engineering, business, computer and life science, healthcare, law, and management. Among those occupations, engineering, life sciences, business, and law have experience the most growth recently.



Houston

Job growth for Houston has increased by 2.8%; adding more than 86,200 jobs to from last year. Engineering and service related jobs increased by 9.5% and business service related jobs increased by 4.6% over the past year. However, the most significant growth has been within the manufacturing industry. Manufacturing job growth contributed 14,900 jobs within the past year; making this the 21st consecutive month for annual job growth.

San Antonio

Job growth for San Antonio has dropped to 1.4% from last year, in comparison to 2.0% from the previous year. However, San Antonio is aiming on becoming the 6th largest city by 2021. There was an increase in construction industry by 5.1%; making it the greatest increase in job growth.

Austin

Job growth for Austin has increased by 2.1%; adding more than 22,700 jobs to from last year. Based on last years' job growth, Austin has became the 16th fastest growing major metropolitan city. Wholesale trade grew by 8.2% making it the fastest growing industry. However, professional and business services added the most 6,000 jobs sparking the greatest increase in job growth.

Dallas

Job growth for Dallas has increased by 3.3%; adding more than 84,900 jobs to from last year. Professional and business service related job grew by 3.8% over the past year. The industry with the greatest increase in job growth was lesiure and hospitality; which added 18,800 jobs.
